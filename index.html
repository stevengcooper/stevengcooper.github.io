About Me

I am a creative person by nature, and that passion for new work has led me to write plays, songs, poetry, produce theatre, create fight choreography for the stage, and teach the theatrical arts to the next generation in the public school system. That creative drive has also helped to fuel my passion for software development.

I never thought that I would embark on a career in development, but I found myself finding the same enjoyment in the creative process in building websites as I did creating on the page or the stage. I discovered this studying alone on Code Academy and Treehouse before I discovered the opportunity to further my education at the Iron Yard. After three months of intensive study I’ve taken many steps forward in learning new languages and tools, but also I’ve learned how to think and research like a developer. I’ve learned a lot, but I’ve also learned how much there is out there to learn in the realm of software development. What at first seemed overwhelming is now exciting to me. Learning how to research and solve problems in languages that are new to me was a great challenge at first, and at times it is still a great challenge now. Knowing how and where to look for answers in the vast expanse on the internet is an education in itself, and I find myself growing stronger at that art daily.

When I’m not studying or working on my apps I find time to play music and work in my garden. It was difficult to balance the 60 - 80 hour weeks of the Iron Yard in the midst of spring planting at the house, but finding an hour here or there to dig in the dirt is a great way to destress and ground myself. I stay active in the theatre and improv community as well in my free time, and when time allows I still audition for films in the area. Without a creative outlet in my life I would be an unhappy person. Life is to short for that.

Latest Projects

The Scenery

The Scenery aims to support the performing arts industry by providing resources for patrons of the arts and artists alike. You can find out about your local performance scene whether you’re a patron looking for fresh entertainment, an actor looking for an audition, or a company looking to promote their work.

New

YThe Scenery utilizes some of the latest web technologies to bring live performance to you! Built with HTML5 and CSS3, using an Angular.js and Jquery front end supported by Ruby on Rails and PostgreSQL database serving up our API. The-Scenery.com is served to all your devices by Heroku and Firebase servers wrapped in one domain with Amazon Web Services providing additional file storage for the images.


The Scenery allows everyone to have the performing arts at their fingertips. Bertolt Brecht once said, “The play isn’t finished until people are in the streets talking about it.” The street corner is now digital, and its bustling live at www.the-scenery.com.
